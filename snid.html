The Sinusoidal Numeric Incremental Devices [SNID] are a class of stand-alone patchable drone synthesizers.

They can generally be identified by their unique wave multiplier voicing structure which is commonly accompanied by a sinusoid generator and frequency divider. While at times the SNID may emit sounds that can be perceived as pleasant, they predominantly generate harsh rhythmic tones on divisions of their voicing frequencies.

Though similar to Phase Distortion [PD], testing has shown the Wave Multiplication Synthesis [WMS] to be highly unstable (Fig. 1).
